Logseq vs Obsidian: Why Obsidian Users Add Naidis

Logseq and Obsidian are both excellent local-first note-taking apps. This isn't about trashing Logseq—it's about helping you understand what Naidis adds if you choose Obsidian.

Why Choose Obsidian?

Users often pick Obsidian for:

  • Larger plugin ecosystem: 1000+ community plugins
  • More flexible structure: Documents, not just outlines
  • Themes and customization: Extensive appearance options
  • Canvas: Visual note mapping
  • Mobile apps: Smoother iOS/Android experience
  • Active development: Regular updates

What Naidis Adds to Obsidian

If you're using or considering Obsidian, Naidis fills gaps that Logseq handles natively—plus adds unique capabilities.

AI Chat (RAG)

Ask questions about your vault. Naidis retrieves relevant notes and generates contextual answers. Use Ollama locally or cloud APIs.

Logseq: Has some AI plugins, but less mature.

YouTube Transcripts

Extract full transcripts with timestamps. Create notes from videos automatically.

Logseq: No native equivalent.

Web Clipping

Capture articles as clean markdown. Better than browser extensions.

Logseq: Basic web capture exists.

PDF Processing

Extract text, tables, and images. OCR for scanned documents.

Logseq: Has PDF annotation, but no extraction/OCR.

Spaced Repetition

Built-in review system with SM-2 or Half-life algorithms. Track streaks and mastery.

Logseq: Has native flashcards, actually quite good.

Kindle Highlights

Import your Kindle highlights directly into Obsidian.

Logseq: No native support.

RSS Reader

Follow feeds directly in Obsidian. Save articles to your vault.

Logseq: Community plugins exist.

Who Should Choose Obsidian + Naidis?

Pick this combo if:

  • ✅ You prefer document-based notes over outlines
  • ✅ You want AI chat with your notes
  • ✅ You capture content from YouTube, web, PDFs
  • ✅ You want Kindle integration
  • ✅ You value the larger plugin ecosystem
  • ✅ You want spaced repetition + content capture in one plugin

Who Should Choose Logseq?

Pick Logseq if:

  • ✅ You think in outlines and bullet points
  • ✅ Built-in PDF annotation is important
  • ✅ You want fully open source
  • ✅ You prefer fewer plugins (more built-in)
  • ✅ You like Logseq's native flashcard system

Both Are Great

This isn't a battle. Both Logseq and Obsidian are excellent local-first tools. They just suit different thinking styles.
